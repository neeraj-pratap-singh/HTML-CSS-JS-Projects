7 Wonders of the World

Taj Mahal

A symbol of love, the Taj Mahal was built by Emperor Shah Jahan. Located in Agra, India, it's a masterpiece of Mughal architecture.

Pyramids of Egypt

The Great Pyramid of Giza is the oldest of the Seven Wonders. Built for Pharaoh Khufu, it showcases the architectural genius of ancient Egypt.

Great Wall of China

Stretching over 13,000 miles, the Great Wall was built to protect China from invasions. It's a remarkable feat of ancient defensive architecture.

Hanging Gardens of Babylon

Said to have been built in ancient Babylon, near present-day Baghdad. These gardens are legendary and their actual existence is still a subject of debate.

Lighthouse of Alexandria

Also known as the Pharos of Alexandria, it guided sailors safely into the busy port of Alexandria, Egypt. It was destroyed in an earthquake in the 14th century.

Statue of Zeus at Olympia

Created by the Greek sculptor Phidias, this statue was erected in the Temple of Zeus. It was made of gold and ivory and was about 12 meters high.

Colossus of Rhodes

This statue of the Greek god Helios stood over 30 meters high. Unfortunately, it was destroyed in an earthquake in 226 BC.
